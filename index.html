Quantum and Linear-Optical Computation

Overview

Quantum information science promises to revolutionize information processing with faster quantum algorithms and novel protocols for cryptography, metrology, and sensing. The Quantum and Linear-Optical Computation group explores quantum theory features enabling quantum information processing tasks, particularly in photonic quantum computers.

Research Lines

Foundations of Quantum Computation

  • Quantification of resources: quantum contextuality, coherence, non-locality, adaptivity, etc.
  • Optimization for Noisy, Intermediate-scale Quantum (NISQ) devices.

Photonic Quantum Computation

  • Requirements for scalable photonic quantum computation.
  • Characterization of multiphoton indistinguishability.
  • Computational uses for multi-mode interferometers.
  • Classical simulation algorithms.

Quantum Software Engineering

  • Semantic structures for various types of classical control and quantum data.
  • Algorithmic calculi for systematic derivation of quantum programs.
  • Dynamic logics for quantum algorithms.
  • Compositional methods for distributed quantum computational systems coordination.
